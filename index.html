Temperature

It refers to the average measurement of kinetic energy generated by individual atoms or molecules of matter. When we put thermal energy into matter. Its atoms move faster. This causes the temperature to rise. Atoms of matter move slower. This causes the temperature to drop.

Degrees Celsius

In 1742 Anders Celsius, Swedish astronomer The thermometer scale has been designed to be easier to read. It has an ice melting point of 0°C and a water boiling point of 100°C.

Fahrenheit degrees

In 1714 German physicist Gabrial Fahrenheit invented the thermometer, which contained mercury in a glass tube. He tried to bring mercury down to a minimum (0°F) using ice and salt mixed with water. He considered the melting point of ice to be 32°F and the boiling point of water to be 212°F.
